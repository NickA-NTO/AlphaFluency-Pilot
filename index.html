AlphaFluency Pilot  ·  Confidential

Executive Summary

10 Students  ·  Grades K–2  ·  One Week  ·  Alpha Schools  ·  March 2026
● Executive Verdict — Effective. Continue and Extend.
AlphaFluency placed 8 of 10 students at the correct reading difficulty in an average of 26 minutes of practice, and 7 of 10 students closed their gap toward their grade-level reading target within the first week. A post-pilot Literably cold-read assessment is required to confirm these gains transfer to independent reading.
Research Findings
Q1 — Adaptivity ● Strong
Did AlphaFluency quickly reach each student's correct difficulty level?
Starting from the lowest possible difficulty, the adaptive engine reached each student's assessed level in an average of 26 minutes of practice for 8 of 10 students. The 2 exceptions are fully explained by insufficient usage time and one student being the highest reader in the cohort.

Did students improve from their own AlphaFluency starting scores?

Q3 — Growth vs. Literably Baseline ● Mixed — Data Gap
Did students improve compared to their cold-read Literably scores?
Only 3 of 10 show verified gains after stripping out the heard-first format advantage (worth ~17 WCPM). Most negative results are explained by the system not yet reaching each student's full difficulty level — not by fluency declining. A post-pilot Literably assessment is the critical missing data point.

Q4 — Grade Target Progress ● On Track
Are students moving toward their grade-level reading benchmarks?
7 of 10 students closed their WCPM gap to grade target, and 6 covered 69–81% of the required difficulty range in a single week. No Grade 2 student has reached ATOS 3.0 yet — expected given all sessions started at 0.4 — but several are one week away from that mark.
