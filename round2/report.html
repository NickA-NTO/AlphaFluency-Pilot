Question 1 — Placement Accuracy

Did AlphaFluency place students at the correct reading difficulty?

● In Progress: 13 of 30 assessable students within ±0.5 ATOS of grade target
Methodology

Definition of “correct difficulty”

A placement is considered near-target if the student’s AF Final ATOS is within ±0.5 of their grade reading target ATOS. All sessions begin at ATOS 0.4; the adaptive engine raises difficulty based on real-time performance.

  • Gap ≤ ±0.5: Near target — green
  • Gap 0.5–1.0: Still progressing — amber
  • Gap > 1.0: Significant gap — red

Early-stage caveat: Most students started between 4–20 March 2026, giving many fewer than two weeks of practice. AF begins conservatively at ATOS 0.4 for all students regardless of prior assessment. Some undershoot at this stage is expected and normal — it reflects the ramp-up arc, not a failure of the adaptive engine.

Question 2 — Growth from AF Baseline

Did students improve from their own AlphaFluency starting scores?

● Positive: 28 of 32 students showed genuine normalised WCPM gains
Methodology

Why raw WCPM cannot be compared directly

Every student starts AF at ATOS 0.4 and progresses to harder texts. A student whose WCPM drops from 80 (at ATOS 0.4) to 60 (at ATOS 2.5) has not regressed — harder text naturally reduces WCPM. We normalise both scores to ATOS 1.0 using the slope of 37 WCPM per 1.0 ATOS level.

Normalisation formula

Normalised WCPM = Actual WCPM + 37 × (Actual ATOS − 1.0)

Since all initial scores are at ATOS 0.4:

Initial Norm = Initial WCPM − 22.2  |  Final Norm = Final WCPM + 37 × (Final ATOS − 1.0)

A positive difference (Final Norm − Initial Norm) means genuine fluency growth above and beyond difficulty increase.

Adjusted Final WCPM — bad-read protection

A student’s last book may be an anomalously poor read. To prevent a single bad session from understating progress, the Final WCPM is floored at the student’s smoothed average:

Adj. Final WCPM = max(Final WCPM, Avg WCPM + 8 × (Avg ATOS − Final ATOS))

The 8 WCPM/ATOS slope (Hasbrouck & Tindal norms) normalises the average session WCPM to the final difficulty level. Students marked § in the table had their adjusted value applied.

Note on non-gainers: All 4 remaining students with negative gains ended at Final ATOS 0.4 with lower WCPM than they started. This typically indicates accuracy issues that prevented AF from advancing them in difficulty. Their normalised gain will be negative whenever Final WCPM < Initial WCPM at the same difficulty level.

Question 3 — Growth vs. Literably Baseline

Did students improve compared to their cold-read Literably scores?

● Mixed — 3 of 30 verified gains above format-inflation threshold
Methodology

The format-inflation problem

AlphaFluency reads text aloud to the student before they read it independently (heard-first). Literably presents completely unseen text (cold read). Heard-first consistently produces higher WCPM than cold read for the same student on the same text — estimated at ∼17 WCPM in this cohort. A raw AF WCPM that exceeds a Literably WCPM is therefore not necessarily a genuine gain.

Normalisation and verification threshold

Both scores are normalised to the student’s AF Final ATOS as the common reference point:

Lit (norm) = Lit WCPM + 37 × (Lit ATOS − AF Final ATOS)

A gain is verified only if: AF Final WCPM > Lit (norm) + 17

This means the student must beat their normalised Literably score by more than the expected heard-first inflation before the gain is considered genuine.

Why so few verified gains? Many students have Literably baselines at ATOS 2.0–3.3, while their AF Final ATOS is still 0.4–2.3. Normalising Literably down to a lower ATOS inflates the Literably score (harder text → higher equivalent at easier text). Until AF difficulty catches up with Literably difficulty, the normalised comparison will remain unfavourable. This is expected at the early pilot stage — not evidence of poor outcomes.

Question 4 — Grade Target Progress

Are students closing the gap toward their grade-level reading target?

● Mixed: 10 of 30 students show gap closure vs. Literably baseline
Methodology

How gap closure is measured

The Literably baseline WCPM is normalised to the student’s AF Final ATOS to create a common difficulty reference. If the student’s AF Final WCPM exceeds this normalised Literably baseline, they have moved closer to their grade benchmark — their gap to target is smaller than when the pilot began.

Lit (norm) = Lit WCPM + 37 × (Lit ATOS − AF Final ATOS)

Gap closed if: AF Final WCPM > Lit (norm)

Why Literably is used as the baseline

Literably is a cold read — the more conservative, harder-to-inflate measure. Using it as the starting point means any gap closure confirmed here is credible signal, not an artefact of heard-first inflation. Note that this comparison has no format-inflation buffer applied — unlike Q3, which required clearing +17 WCPM. Gap closure here is the lower bar: AF must simply beat the normalised Literably WCPM.

Interpretation note: Many students have Literably baselines at harder ATOS levels than their current AF Final ATOS. Normalising Literably down to the lower AF ATOS inflates the Literably benchmark, making gap closure harder to show even if real growth has occurred. As AF difficulty rises to match Literably difficulty, gap closure numbers will improve.

Key Findings
  • ●
    Students with lower Literably ATOS baselines are most likely to show gap closure. The 10 students who closed their gap include those with Literably at ATOS 0.2–2.0. Students with Literably at 3.0–3.3 (Ren Sticker, Mya Hadnot, Blake Price) show large negative gaps because the normalisation amplifies a high Literably score when AF hasn’t yet reached that difficulty.
  • ●
    Aiden Richardson and Bandon Peterson are borderline cases — gaps of just −1.7 and −1.8 WCPM respectively. Both would likely show gap closure with a few more sessions. Aiden in particular has 100 minutes invested and is trending upward.
  • ●
    Ren Sticker’s −53.6 gap is an artefact of their very high Literably score (84 WCPM at ATOS 3.3). When normalised to AF Final ATOS 2.1, the Literably benchmark becomes 128.4 WCPM — an extremely high bar that AF is unlikely to clear until difficulty is fully matched. This does not mean Ren has declined; it means the comparison is unfair until AF reaches ATOS 3.3.
  • ●
    Recommended action: Re-run Q4 at 6 weeks. As AF difficulty catches up to Literably difficulty for the advanced cohort, gap closure rates are expected to improve substantially. A Literably re-test at the same time will provide the cleanest cross-format comparison.
